Vectors

What is a Vector?

A vector is a quantity that has both magnitude and direction. Represented typically with an arrow: length shows magnitude and the arrowhead shows direction.

Examples of vectors: displacement, velocity, acceleration, force, momentum.

What is a Scalar Quantity?

A scalar quantity has only magnitude, no direction.

Examples: mass, time, temperature, speed.

How to Represent a Vector

Vectors can be represented:

  • Graphically – using arrows
  • With notations – using components (2D for IJSO)

Components of a Vector

If a vector has magnitude \(A\) and angle \(\theta\) from horizontal, then it's components are

  • Horizontal component (x): \( A_x = A \cos(\theta) \)
  • Vertical component (y):\(A_y = A \sin(\theta) \)

Expressed with unit vectors: \(A_x \hat{i} + A_y \hat{j}\)

Addition of Vectors Using Components

  • \(R_x = A_x + B_x\)
  • \(R_y = A_y + B_y\)
  • \( R = \sqrt{R_x^2 + R_y^2} \)
  • \(θ = \tan^{-1}(\frac{R_y}{R_x})\)

A vector 𝐀 has a magnitude of 5 units and is directed along the positive x-axis. A vector 𝐁 has a magnitude of 12 units and is directed along the positive y-axis. Find the magnitude and direction of A + B

  • Vector A: 5 units along x-axis
  • Vector B: 12 units along y-axis
  • Resultant: \(R = \sqrt{5^2 + 12^2} = 13\)
  • Angle: \(θ = \tan^{-1}(\frac{12}{5}) \approx 67.4^{\circ}\)

Determine the x and y components of a displacement whose magnitude is 30.0 m at a 23° angle from the x-axis.

  • x component: \(A_x = 30 \cos(23^{\circ}) \approx 27.62 N\)
  • y component: \(A_y = 30 \sin(23^{\circ}) \approx 11.72 N\)

A force of 15 N acts in the direction 45° above the positive x-axis, and another force of 10 N acts 45° above the positive x-axis. Find the resultant force and direction.

  • First, we will write both vectors in terms of their x-axis and y-axis components.
  • \(A_x = 15 \cos(45^{\circ}) \approx 10.61 N\)
  • \(A_y = 15 \sin(45^{\circ}) \approx 10.61 N\)
  • \(B_x = 10 \cos(30^{\circ}) \approx 8.66 N\)
  • \(B_y = 10 \sin(30^{\circ}) = 5 N\)
  • Now we will add the components to find the components of the resultant vector
  • \(R_x = 10.61 + 8.66 = 19.27 N\)
  • \(R_y = 10.61 + 5 = 15.61 N\)
  • Now we will find the resultant vector and its magnitude
  • Resultant: \(R = \sqrt{19.27^2 + 15.61^2} \approx 24.8 N\)
  • Angle: \(\theta = \tan^{-1}(\frac{15.61}{19.27}) \approx 39.0^{\circ}\)

Vector D = 3i - 4j and vector: E = 4i - j. Find the magnitude of D + E and the magnitude of D - E.

  • We can add the add the two unit vectors
  • \((3 + 4)\hat{i} = 7\hat{i}\)
  • \((-4 + (-1))\hat{j} = -5\hat{j}\)
  • \(D + E = 7\hat{i} - 5\hat{j}\)
  • Now we can find the magnitude of this vector
  • \(|D + E| = \sqrt{7^2 + (-5)^2}\)
  • Magnitude: \(|D + E| = 8.60 N\)
  • Now, we can subtract the vector E from vector D
  • \((3 - 4)\hat{i} = -1\hat{i}\)
  • \(((-4) - (-1))\hat{j} = -3\hat{j}\)
  • \(D - E = -1\hat{i} - 3\hat{j}\)
  • Now we can find the magnitude of this vector
  • \(|D - E| = \sqrt{(-1)^2 + (-3)^2}\)
  • Magnitude: \(|D - E| = 3.16 N\)

A student carries a lump of clay from the first floor (ground level) door of a skyscraper (on Verstappen Street) to the elevator, 30 m away. She then takes the elevator to the 11th floor. Finally, she exits the elevator and carries the clay 15 m back toward Verstappen Street. Determine the total displacement for the clay if each floor is 4.5 m above the floor below.

  • First, we will find the vertical displacement
  • \(A_x = 4.5 \times 10 = 45m\)
  • Now, we will find the horizontal displacement
  • \(A_y = 30 - 15 = 15m\)
  • Now, we will find the resultant displacement
  • Resultant: \(R = \sqrt{45^2 + 15^2} \approx 47.43m\)
